About This Project

The Digital Literacy Program at the Columbia Center for Teaching and Learning

The Digital Literacy Competency Calculator is part of the Digital Literacy for Instructional Practices Program, a collaboration between the Columbia University Center for Teaching and Learning and Columbia University Libraries. Our program recognizes that, as is the case with print literacy, there is no single digital literacy, no one set of skills that can be mastered in a single course of study. Rather, digital literacy is characterized by the ability to self-consciously deploy operations such as critical thinking and communications through cognitive processes that are unique to the digital realm. Disciplines and professions have also begun to develop further specialized applications of these operations that are known as digital competencies.

We have been encouraged to see that some of the original efforts at articulating digital literacy standards, the JISC and Mozilla Web Literacy initiatives, are pursuing versions of this approach as well by including teaching materials and processes by which their standards can be implemented. Our thinking has also been guided and reinforced by Doug Belshaw’s work on digital literacy as a spectrum of development that moves upward from skills to critical fluencies and Rebecca Reynolds’ notion of a framework for a “social constructivist digital literacy”.*

Collaboration and revision are essential to the success of this method, which is why we have chosen the Git Hub platform to host and disseminate the work. We hope to learn from others’ adaptations of this representation format, and to assemble a gallery of work that begins to help us understand what a comprehensive set of digital literacy instructional practices might look like if built from the ground up.

The Digital Literacy Competency Calculator is a collaboration between the Columbia University Libraries and Center for Teaching and Learning. Contributors to the first release of this project include Amy Nurnburger, Simone Sacchi, Susan Dreher, Marc Raymond, Zachary Kornhauser, Jessica Brodsky, and Lucy Appert.
