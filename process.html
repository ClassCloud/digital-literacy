Instructional Design Process

Learn about our process and templates.

The Competency Calculator standards representation is an integral part of this instructional design process for digital literacy developed at the Columbia Center for Teaching and Learning. All templates can be found on our program’s Resources page. We also maintain a Reading List.

Learning Design

  • Select an appropriate cognitive, disciplinary, or professional competency standard for alignment
  • Select the elements of the standard to be covered in the course or learning experience and add any customizations or specifications to the chosen standard. For example, DH instructors may add specific questions pertinent to their learning experience such as “What is a book?” to the Intellectual competencies for their course. Breaking down the ACRL standard to specific goals and ideas is essential because it is so broad.
  • Using our design rubric, determine the level of competency on the modified Bloom’s scale to be covered in the learning experience.
  • Pair each competency and each level within it to a specific learning activity that will allow students to demonstrate mastery. (See metadata example)
  • Iterate on syllabus design and competency selection/customization as many times as necessary to produce a coherent, well-scaffolded learning experience.
  • Develop a pre/post familiarity survey based on the competencies to be covered in the learning experience (see survey templates on our Resources page).

Instructional Implementation

  • Post the syllabus design rubric and competencies representation map on the course website
  • Distribute pre surveys
  • Use pre survey results to adjust learning design. Learners may be more advanced than instructor assumed or they may need more prerequisites; there may be the need for more individualized instruction.
  • As the course progresses, note any additional skills or competencies that are developed, need to be developed, or emerge as intellectual agenda items.
  • Distribute post surveys
  • Assess efficacy of instructional plan based on student work and survey results
  • Iterate again on learning design

Reporting Out

Share Competencies Calculator for learning experience back to community with notes on what worked and what did not.
