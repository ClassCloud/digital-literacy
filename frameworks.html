Frameworks

Get the code to create your own customized version of the Competency Calculator.

On this site, you will find examples of our learning design process and our prototype representation format, the Competency Calculator, that have been adapted for three different types of digital literacy frameworks.

All of these adaptations require further customizations based on the instructional plan for each learning experience. Our model allows instructors to select only the skills being covered in their course or bootcamp from the available skills in each standard and also to add new ones.

For example, in the case of the Intellectual competency area for the Digital Humanities standard, there is much work to be done to fill in the categories of new knowledge being created through the complex digital interactions in which students engage in these courses. We think the growth of a list of these categories will strengthen arguments about the intellectual value of digital humanities courses

Let's Get Started

ACRL’s 2016 Information Literacy Standard

As the broadest and most conceptual of the frameworks our faculty have worked with, we have found the ACRL Standard most helpful for faculty who are able to integrate the information literacy thresholds and their digital skill components into their instruction of disciplinary conventions. We will be excited to see how others break these thresholds down along the Bloom’s scale and pair them with activities to create discrete competencies.

Digital Humanities

Schnapp et al’s MIT Short Guide to Digital Humanities outlines a framework for competencies and learning outcomes that we find particularly helpful for the presentation of not just digital skills to be mastered but more importantly of the crucial merging of those skills into the intellectual work of the course or learning experience. We believe the division of competencies into Technical, Administrative, and Intellectual can be helpful for the formation or revision of other disciplinary or professional digital literacy standards.

Explore Framework

Eshet

Yoram Eshet’s cognitive skills framework (rev. 2012) posits six skills that are unique to the digital realm. We find it provides an excellent framework for representing the kinds of general digital skills and methods now commonly taught as part of many educational experiences. To help instructors apply this framework to their work, we have created a version built out along our modified three Bloom’s levels and provided examples of common activities at each level.
